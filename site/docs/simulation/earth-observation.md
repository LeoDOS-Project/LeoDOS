# Earth Observation

The simulation supports four types of Earth observation imagery. Each can be provided as synthetic data (generated by the `eosim` tool) or as real archived satellite data replayed through the pipeline.

## Optical

Visible-light imagery captured by a camera (simulated by the Arducam OV5640). Optical images depend on daylight and clear skies — they cannot see through clouds or at night. Resolution and coverage depend on the camera optics and orbital altitude. Optical data is used for visual inspection, land cover classification, and change detection in well-lit conditions.

## Thermal IR

Brightness temperature images in the mid-wave infrared (MWIR, ~3.9 μm) and long-wave infrared (LWIR, ~11 μm) bands. Thermal IR does not depend on sunlight — it measures heat radiated by the surface, so it works at night and through thin cloud. A fire at ~600 K stands out clearly against a ~300 K background. Thermal data is the primary input for [wildfire detection](/spacecomp/use-cases/wildfire) workflows.

**Synthetic:** `eosim` generates brightness temperature rasters with a diurnal background model, spatial variation, sensor noise, and configurable fire events (location, onset, peak temperature, spread rate).

**Real data:** MODIS and VIIRS thermal bands provide global coverage at 375 m–1 km resolution with multiple daily revisits.

## SAR

Synthetic Aperture Radar transmits microwave pulses and records the reflected signal. SAR works through clouds, at night, and in all weather — it does not depend on sunlight or clear skies. SAR data comes in two forms:

- **Complex (SLC)** — preserves both amplitude and phase. Phase differences between repeat passes reveal sub-millimeter surface displacement (InSAR), used for [tailings dam](/spacecomp/use-cases/tailings-dam) and [volcanic](/spacecomp/use-cases/volcanic) monitoring.
- **Backscatter (intensity)** — measures how strongly the surface reflects radar. Calm water appears dark (specular reflection away from the sensor), making oil spills and flood extent visible as dark patches against rough-surface surroundings. Used for [oil spill](/spacecomp/use-cases/oil-spill), [flood](/spacecomp/use-cases/flood), and [sea ice](/spacecomp/use-cases/sea-ice) detection.

**Synthetic:** `eosim` generates SLC images with displacement phase screens (for InSAR) and backscatter images with dark-spot injection (for oil spill/flood).

**Real data:** Sentinel-1 provides free, global C-band SAR at 5×20 m resolution with a 6-day revisit cycle.

## Multispectral

Images captured in multiple wavelength bands (visible, near-infrared, shortwave infrared). The key derived product is NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index), which measures vegetation health by comparing red and near-infrared reflectance. Healthy vegetation reflects strongly in NIR and absorbs red; bare soil or dead vegetation does not. A drop in NDVI over time indicates [deforestation](/spacecomp/use-cases/deforestation) or crop loss.

**Synthetic:** `eosim` generates NDVI maps with per-land-cover baselines and deforestation polygons that expand over successive passes.

**Real data:** Landsat (30 m, 16-day revisit) and Sentinel-2 (10 m, 5-day revisit) provide free global multispectral imagery.
