# Overview

This section provides context for readers who are not from the space domain. It covers the physical environment that LeoDOS operates in — orbits, constellations, links, sensors, and the constraints they impose on software and hardware.

- [Orbits](orbits) — orbital parameters, altitude classes, perturbations, propagation methods, and attitude
- [Constellations](constellations) — Walker Delta geometry, phasing, real-world examples
- [Satellite ↔ Satellite Links](satellite-links) — inter-satellite link technologies, distance and throughput, optical ISLs vs fiber
- [Ground ↔ Satellite Links](ground-links) — ground station passes, throughput by band, cross-orbit relay
- [Sensors](sensors) — optical, SAR, thermal IR, multispectral, and hyperspectral Earth observation
- [Threats](threats) — the downlink wall, radiation, atmospheric drag, and orbital debris
- [Hardware](hardware) — radiation-hardened processors, memory, storage, and power
- [Software](software) — flight software frameworks, real-time operating systems, and LeoDOS
